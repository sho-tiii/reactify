Discovery

Discovered by Jöns Jacob Berzelius in 1828.

Common Uses

  • Nuclear fuel in reactors
  • Manufacture of high-quality lenses
  • Alloying element in magnesium

Interesting Facts

  • Thorium is more abundant in Earth's crust than uranium.
  • It has potential as a safer alternative to uranium in nuclear reactors.
  • Thorium dioxide has the highest melting point of any oxide.
